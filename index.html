§ 05 — Writing

The Palate and the Harness.

Featured framework · Developer Palate

The chef who can taste. The line cook who only follows recipes.

AI widened the aperture for anyone who can write code. The question is no longer can you cook — everyone with an IDE can cook. The question is whether you have a palate. Whether you can taste the dish before the customer does.

Five essays. Starts with the distinction, ends with how to train the taste buds. Read it before hiring your next engineer.

5 parts · 28 min read · Published July 2025

The Chef  

  • Tastes the dish mid-flight.
  • Changes the recipe when the stock is off.
  • Knows why acid cuts fat.
  • Hires for palate, not speed.
  • Says "no" to the customer.

vs. The Line Cook

  • Follows the ticket.
  • Plates what's on the recipe card.
  • Confuses speed with craft.
  • Scales by adding stations.
  • Says "yes, chef" to everything.
